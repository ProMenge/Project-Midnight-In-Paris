Owen Wilson as Gil Pender

Owen Wilson plays Gil Pender, a screenwriter and aspiring novelist who is deeply nostalgic for the Paris of the 1920s. He is enchanted by the city's historical charm and is in search of inspiration for his first novel. Gil's romantic idealism and yearning for a different time period are central to his character's journey as he navigates through the cultural and artistic wonders of the past while questioning his present life choices and relationship.

Rachel McAdams as Inez

Rachel McAdams portrays Inez, Gil's pragmatic and materialistic fiancée. Unlike Gil, Inez is not captivated by the allure of Paris's historical and cultural past. She prefers the comforts of modern life and is more interested in social status and material wealth. Her character often clashes with Gil's romantic and idealistic notions, providing a stark contrast that highlights the differences in their worldviews and aspirations.

Marion Cotillard as Adriana

Marion Cotillard plays Adriana, a beautiful and mysterious muse from the 1920s who becomes romantically involved with several prominent artists of the era. She embodies the glamorous and artistic spirit of the time, and her relationships with various creative figures highlight her allure and influence. Adriana's character represents the idealized past that Gil longs for, and her presence in his life brings both inspiration and complications as he grapples with his desires and the realities of his own time.

Corey Stoll as Ernest Hemingway

Corey Stoll takes on the role of Ernest Hemingway, the iconic American writer known for his direct writing style and adventurous spirit. Hemingway becomes a mentor figure to Gil, offering him advice on writing, life, and love. Through his interactions with Hemingway, Gil gains valuable insights and begins to understand the importance of living passionately and authentically. Hemingway's larger-than-life personality and wisdom play a crucial role in shaping Gil's journey and self-discovery.
